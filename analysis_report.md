# Amazon Sales Report Analysis

## Executive Summary
This report presents a comprehensive analysis of the Amazon sales dataset. The analysis covers sales trends, product performance, fulfillment effectiveness, customer segmentation, and geographical distribution.

**Key Findings:**
*   **Total Sales:** INR 78,590,170.25 (approx 78.6 Million)
*   **Total Orders:** 120,229
*   **Average Order Value:** INR 653.67
*   **Cancelled Orders:** 18,332 (approx 15% of total orders)

## 1. Sales Overview
The daily sales trend shows fluctuations over the analyzed period. There are specific peaks that might correspond to promotions or weekends.
![Sales Trend](analysis_results/sales_trend.png)

## 2. Product Analysis
### Category Performance
The 'Set' and 'Kurta' categories are the top performers in terms of sales revenue, followed by 'Western Dress' and 'Top'.
![Category Sales](analysis_results/category_sales.png)

### Size Distribution
The most popular sizes are M, L, and XL, indicating a high demand for standard sizes.
![Size Distribution](analysis_results/size_distribution.png)

## 3. Fulfillment Analysis
A significant portion of orders are fulfilled by Amazon (FBA), which generally ensures faster delivery. However, there is a notable number of 'Merchant' fulfilled orders. The cancellation rate seems distributed across both, but further deep dive could reveal if one method has higher cancellations.
![Fulfillment Status](analysis_results/fulfillment_status.png)

## 4. Geographical Analysis
Maharashtra and Karnataka are the top contributing states to sales, followed by Uttar Pradesh and Telangana. This suggests a strong customer base in these regions, likely driven by major metros like Mumbai and Bangalore.
![State Sales](analysis_results/state_sales.png)

## 5. Customer Segmentation (B2B vs B2C)
The vast majority of sales are B2C (Business to Consumer), with B2B (Business to Business) making up a very small fraction (less than 1%).
![B2B Split](analysis_results/b2b_split.png)

## Recommendations
1.  **Inventory Management**: Stock up on top-selling categories (Sets, Kurtas) and sizes (M, L, XL) to avoid stockouts.
2.  **Regional Marketing**: Focus marketing campaigns in Maharashtra and Karnataka to leverage existing strong presence. Consider targeted campaigns in emerging states to boost sales.
3.  **Fulfillment Optimization**: Analyze the reasons for cancellations, especially for Merchant-fulfilled orders, to improve fulfillment rates.
4.  **B2B Expansion**: There is a huge potential for growth in the B2B segment. tailored strategies for bulk buyers could unlock new revenue streams.
